P — Publishing / Rights

Know what
you own

Before you sign anything, license anything, or partner with anyone — you need to understand your rights.

What Most Artists
Don't Know

  • ●The difference between master rights and publishing rights
  • ●What a sync license actually involves and who controls it
  • ●How performance rights work and why PRO registration matters
  • ●When and how rights can be signed away unknowingly

DGP Rights Education

"Rights education isn't legal advice. It's foundational knowledge every artist needs before they make any deal."

Rights Covered

Master Rights

The recording itself — who owns it, what it means, and how it affects streaming royalties and licensing opportunities.

Publishing Rights

The composition — the melody and lyrics. How publishing is split, registered, and monetized through mechanical and performance royalties.

Sync Rights

How your music can be licensed for use in TV, film, ads, and games. Who controls sync, and what a sync deal actually involves for the artist.

Performance Rights

The royalties collected when your music is performed, broadcast, or streamed publicly — and how PRO registration ensures you receive them.

P — Publishing / Royalties

Collect what
you're already owed

Most artists are leaving royalty income uncollected. DGP structures everything so nothing falls through the cracks.

Where Royalties
Get Lost

  • ●No PRO registration — performance royalties never collected
  • ●Mechanical royalties unclaimed due to improper metadata
  • ●International royalties never repatriated
  • ●Split sheets missing — disputes before a track even earns

DGP's Goal

"Set up the infrastructure once. Then let your music earn what it should — without chasing it."

What Gets Structured

PRO Registration

Register your works with the right Performing Rights Organization. Ensure every public performance and broadcast of your music generates the royalties it's entitled to.

Mechanical Setup

Correct metadata, ISRC registration, and mechanical licensing setup — so every stream and download generates the mechanical royalties owed to the composition.

Split Documentation

Written, signed split sheets for every collaborator on every track. Before any release. No assumptions. No disputes after the fact.
